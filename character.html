Character Creation

Below is the first page of the character sheet for a personal level 1 character called MULK created on D&D Beyond.

Click on a boxed part of the sheet to find out more!

Identity

Being a goliath, MULK thinks he is more interesting than a mere human!
He is proud of all the training he did to become a barbarian.
He continues to train hard so that he may become a barbarian of a higher level!

Ability Scores

The six ability scores paint a picture of a character's physical and mental traits.
MULK might not be as charismatic as a bard but he's certain he's strong enough to beat one at arm wrestling!
There are a few ways of setting your character's abilities, rolling for stats being the most exciting one!
How to roll for stats:

  • Roll 4d6 (four six-sided dice).
  • Note down the sum of the highest three rolls.
  • Repeat the above five more times.
  • You now have six numbers, and you can assign each to a different ability score as you wish.

To get the corresponding modifier, take away 10 from an ability score, divide the outcome by 2 and round down the answer if required.
With an impressive score of 17, MULK's strength modifier is +3!
See Saving Throws and Skills for more insight on modifiers.

Saving Throws and Skills

Skills determine whether a character is likely to perform well or not when attempting a task.
Each skill is based on an ability, so, e.g., MULK's strength means that he is very athletic!
Saving throws are made to defend against threats.
When a player performs a skill check or a saving throw, they roll 1d20 and add the relevant modifier to the outcome.
The higher their score is the more likely that their character will be successful.
If the character is proficient in a skill or a saving throw, then the player can also add the character's Proficiency Bonus to the roll.

Initiative

When the players are in a hostile situation, everyone involved in the encounter must roll initiative before it begins.
This determines the order in which the combatants take turns, with the combatant with the highest initiative check getting to act first.
Rolling for initiative is a Dexterity check, hence MULK's initiative score will be determined as 1d20+2.

Armour Class

Armour Class, or AC, represents how well the character will be able to avoid getting hit in battle.
Without armour or shield, AC is calculated as 10 + Dexterity modifier.
However since MULK is a Barbarian (see Unarmored Defense), if he doesn't wear any armour he also gets to add his Constitution modifier, giving a total AC of 16!

Proficiency Bonus

Proficiency Bonus increases at certain levels and can added to numbers when dealing with things the character is proficient in.
Note that MULK is proficient at using martial weapons (see the PROFICIENCIES AND LANGUAGES box in the character sheet).
Hence when the player rolls 1d20 to attack with MULK's greataxe, they can add +2, the Proficiency Bonus, to the roll.
Greataxe is a melee weapon, so +3, the Strength modifier, is also added to attack rolls (the Dexterity modifier is added when attacking with ranged weapons).
Therefore, +5 can be added to attack rolls for MULK's greataxe (see the +5 next to Greataxe in the WEAPON ATTACKS & CANTRIPS box in the character sheet).
If 1d20+5 is greater than or equal to the target's AC then the attack will hit.
Yup! MULK is pretty confident about landing hits with his greataxe!

Managing HP

A character's HP decrease in line with the damage they take.
The character goes down if they lose all their HP. They will then need to make one death save each turn.
This involves rolling 1d20:

  • A roll of 10 or above counts as a success.
  • A roll lower than 10 is a failure.
  • 3 successes make the character stable; they will remain unconscious but won't need to make any more death saves.
  • 3 failures mean death.

A character has a number of hit dice equal to their current level available to them.
After a short rest, the character can spend any number of their hit dice to gain lost HP.
The character is able to regain some of their spent hit dice after a long rest.

Actions

At each turn during combat, the character can do the following:

  • Move a distance up to their speed. MULK can walk up to 30ft each turn (see the SPEED box in the character sheet).
  • Take one action; for MULK it's usually attacking with his greataxe!.
  • Take a bonus action. You wouldn't want to be up against MULK when he enters Rage!
  • Take a reaction. This can also be taken during someone else's turn. After taking a reaction, another can't be taken until the next turn.
